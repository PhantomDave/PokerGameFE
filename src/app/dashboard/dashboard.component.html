Texas Hold'em Rules

  1. Objective: The goal is to have the best hand at showdown or to make opponents fold.
  2. Starting Hands: Each player is dealt two private cards (hole cards), and five community cards are dealt face up in the center.
  3. Betting Rounds: Four rounds â€“ Preflop, Flop, Turn, and River. Players can check, bet, raise, or fold.
  4. Community Cards:
    • Flop: Three community cards are dealt.
    • Turn: A fourth community card is dealt.
    • River: The fifth and final community card is dealt.
  5. Hand Rankings: Standard poker hand rankings apply.
  6. Betting Options:
    • Check: Pass the action to the next player.
    • Bet: Place a wager.
    • Raise: Increase the bet.
    • Fold: Discard the hand and forfeit the round.
  7. Blinds and Antes: Players post blinds and, in some games, antes to initiate betting.
  8. Showdown: If more than one player remains after the final betting round, there's a showdown. The best hand wins the pot.
  9. Winning the Pot: The player with the best hand or the last remaining player wins the pot.
  10. Button and Rotation: The dealer button rotates clockwise after each hand.
